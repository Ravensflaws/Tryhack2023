Benefits

Stress Reduction

When individuals engage in controlled, deep breathing, it signals to the brain that they are in a state of calm, triggering the release of endorphins and reducing the production of stress hormones like cortisol.

The "National Institute of Mental Health" highlights deep breathing as one of the effective coping strategies for managing stress and improving emotional well-being.

Lower Blood Pressure

Deep breathing can promote vasodilation, which is the widening of blood vessels leading to lower resistance of blood flow.

People experiencing anxiety lower their blood pressure by 30 points or more by doing some deep breathing.

Better sleep        

During controlled and deep breathing, Individuals activate the body's relaxation response, lowering stress hormones and alleviating tension, helping them to fall alseep faster

A study published in the "Journal of Clinical Psychology" in 2015 reported that mindfulness meditation, which often incorporates controlled breathing, was associated with improved sleep quality in individuals with insomnia.
